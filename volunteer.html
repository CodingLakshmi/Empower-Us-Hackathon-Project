Our Vision

As a volunteer, you will be paired with a victim of trauma.

Studies have shown that talking about our problems and sharing our negative emotions with someone can be profoundly healing.

Many people live with the ongoing effects of past and present overwhelming stress. We know that people who have not received the support they need to recover are often left struggling with their health and well-being.

Together, we can lend a helping hand to those who went through tough times, and help them cope with their trauma. With these small acts of kindness, we can change someone’s life for the better.

With volunteers, we can help women in need cope with their trauma










Talking about Trauma

When talking, your approach should be empathic and you should consider what must have happened to them. Listen to them, and don’t talk over them.

Try to recognize signs of stress, and help them calms down. If they tell you that they are ‘okay’ but you are still concerned, gently ask them one more time.

Do not give them advice if they do not ask for it. Inquire who they might contact for support. Make sure that they don’t leave in a distressed state.

As one, we can assist these victims of violence empower them to live their lives freely.
